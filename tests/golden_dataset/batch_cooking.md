# Batch Cooking Techniques

## Overview

Batch cooking concentrates food preparation into a single dedicated session, typically one to two hours per week, that produces components and complete dishes for multiple subsequent meals. The efficiency gains come from concentrated setup and cleanup, larger cooking quantities that improve heat distribution, and the elimination of nightly decision-making about what to cook.

## Planning a Batch Cook Session

### Component vs. Complete Dish Strategy

Component cooking prepares versatile building blocks — cooked grains, roasted vegetables, poached or roasted proteins, sauces, dressings — that can be combined in different ways throughout the week. This provides variety; the same roasted vegetables appear in grain bowls on Monday, tacos on Tuesday, and frittata on Wednesday.

Complete dish cooking prepares finished meals that need only reheating. This is faster at mealtime but produces less variety. A hybrid approach — some complete dishes for the busiest evenings, components for everything else — suits most households.

## High-Efficiency Batch Cook Techniques

### Oven Management

Multiple sheet pans can cook simultaneously in the oven: protein on one pan, two pans of vegetables on the racks below. Rotating pans halfway through cooking ensures even browning. Starting with items requiring the highest temperature and reducing heat as each finishes minimizes oven reprogramming.

### Stovetop Multitasking

While the oven runs, the stovetop handles grains and legumes. Rice, quinoa, farro, and lentils can all cook simultaneously in separate pots. A pressure cooker reduces legume cooking time from 90 minutes to 15. Timing all stovetop items to finish together requires only monitoring clocks rather than constant attention.

## Storage and Shelf Life

Cooked grains and legumes store for five days refrigerated. Roasted vegetables keep for four days. Cooked proteins keep for three to four days. Sauces and dressings vary widely by acidity and composition. Labeling containers with contents and date prevents mystery containers and food waste. Freezing excess beyond what will be consumed within the safe window extends utility.
