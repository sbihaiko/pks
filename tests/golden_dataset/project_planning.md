# Personal Project Planning

## Overview

Personal project planning applies the discipline of project management to individual goals and multi-step endeavors. Without a plan, complex projects stall at ambiguity or expand indefinitely through scope creep. A simple but complete plan — defined outcome, identified tasks, realistic timeline, known dependencies — dramatically increases completion rates.

## Defining the Project

A project has a clear outcome that can be declared done. "Improve my Spanish" is not a project; "Complete Duolingo Spanish course through level B1 by June 30" is a project. The outcome should be specific enough that it can unambiguously be achieved or not achieved. Vagueness enables self-deception about progress.

### Work Breakdown Structure

A work breakdown structure (WBS) decomposes the project outcome into progressively smaller components until individual tasks are concrete and actionable — completable in a single work session. Tasks should be sized so that a full day of work completes between two and five of them. Tasks that are too large invite procrastination; tasks that are too small create planning overhead.

## Scheduling and Dependencies

After identifying all tasks, mapping their dependencies reveals the critical path — the sequence of tasks that determines the minimum project duration. Slack time is the amount of delay a non-critical-path task can absorb without delaying the project. Scheduling backwards from the deadline and building in buffer for the critical path produces a realistic timeline.

## Tracking Progress

A simple Kanban board — columns for Backlog, In Progress, Done — provides a visual status view at a glance. Weekly project reviews assess what was completed, what is blocked, and whether the timeline remains realistic. Honest assessment of timeline slippage early allows course correction before deadlines are missed.

## Managing Scope

New ideas during a project are captured in a separate "future considerations" list rather than added immediately. Evaluating additions at weekly reviews, rather than adding them ad hoc, prevents scope expansion from derailing completion. Every addition to scope either extends the deadline or requires removing something else.
