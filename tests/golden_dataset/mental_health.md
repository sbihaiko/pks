# Mental Health and Emotional Wellbeing

## Overview

Mental health encompasses emotional regulation, cognitive function, and the capacity to maintain meaningful relationships and participate in daily life. It is not merely the absence of disorder but a positive state of psychological resilience and purposeful engagement.

## Common Mental Health Challenges in Aging

Depression and anxiety are the most prevalent mental health conditions among older adults, yet they are frequently underdiagnosed because symptoms are attributed to normal aging or physical illness. Screening tools such as the PHQ-9 and GAD-7 provide structured baselines for clinical conversations.

### Grief and Loss

Older adults face accumulating losses—of spouses, peers, physical capacity, and roles. Grief is a natural response, but when it persists beyond expected duration or impairs daily functioning, professional support becomes important. Complicated grief is a distinct condition requiring specialized intervention.

## Protective Factors

### Sense of Purpose

Having meaningful goals and roles correlates strongly with psychological wellbeing and longevity. Volunteering, mentoring, creative pursuits, and community involvement all provide purpose. The specific activity matters less than the sense of contributing to something beyond oneself.

### Autonomy and Agency

Perceived control over one's life circumstances is a robust predictor of mental health in older adults. Supporting decision-making capacity and avoiding unnecessary paternalism in care settings preserves this sense of agency.

## Seeking Help

Mental health treatment is effective at any age. Cognitive-behavioral therapy shows strong evidence for depression and anxiety in older populations. Medication management requires particular care due to polypharmacy risks and altered drug metabolism in aging.

### Reducing Stigma

Many older adults from earlier generations hold stigmatized views of mental health treatment. Framing support as a form of health maintenance—analogous to physical therapy—can lower resistance to engagement.
