# Essential Knife Skills

## Overview

Knife skills are the foundation of efficient cooking. Proficiency with a chef's knife reduces preparation time, produces more uniform pieces that cook evenly, and is safer than working with a dull or mishandled knife. Most home cooks significantly underestimate how much their cooking would improve with better knife technique.

## The Chef's Knife

A chef's knife with an 8-inch blade handles the vast majority of kitchen cutting tasks. The blade should be sharp enough to slice a ripe tomato with minimal downward pressure. A sharp knife requires less force and is safer than a dull one because it does not slip. Honing steel realigns the blade edge; sharpening stone removes metal to restore a damaged edge.

## Fundamental Cuts

### Rocking Motion

The tip of the chef's knife stays in contact with the cutting board while the heel rocks down through the food. The guiding hand forms a claw, fingertips curled under with knuckles angled outward to guide the blade and protect fingertips. The knife side rests against the knuckles; the cutting edge never contacts fingers.

### Julienne

Julienne produces uniform matchstick strips approximately 3mm × 3mm × 5cm. The vegetable is first cut into slabs of the desired thickness, then the slabs are stacked and cut into strips. Uniformity ensures even cooking; thick and thin pieces in the same pan cook at different rates.

### Brunoise

Brunoise is a fine dice derived from julienne: julienned strips are cut crosswise at uniform intervals to produce cubes approximately 3mm on each side. Brunoise vegetables cook quickly, integrate smoothly into sauces, and provide textural interest without dominating a dish.

## Knife Maintenance

A blade that is honed before each use retains its edge far longer than one that is only sharpened when visibly dull. Washing knives by hand rather than in the dishwasher prevents edge damage and handle deterioration. Storing knives on a magnetic strip rather than loose in a drawer prevents dulling through contact with other utensils.
