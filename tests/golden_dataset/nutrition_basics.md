# Nutrition Basics for Healthy Living

## Overview

Good nutrition provides the raw materials for cellular repair, immune defense, and sustained energy. Despite the abundance of dietary advice, the core principles remain consistent: eat mostly whole foods, maintain adequate protein intake, and minimize ultra-processed products.

## Macronutrients

Every diet is built on three macronutrient categories. Understanding their roles helps individuals make informed choices rather than following prescriptive rules.

### Protein

Protein supports muscle maintenance, immune function, and enzyme production. Older adults require 1.2 to 1.6 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily—significantly more than younger adults—because of reduced anabolic efficiency. High-quality sources include eggs, legumes, fish, poultry, and dairy.

### Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's preferred energy substrate. The glycemic index of a food indicates how rapidly it raises blood glucose. Whole grains, vegetables, and legumes provide complex carbohydrates that sustain energy without sharp glucose spikes.

### Fats

Dietary fat is essential for absorbing fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) and for hormonal regulation. Unsaturated fats from olive oil, avocados, and fatty fish are cardioprotective. Limiting saturated fat and eliminating trans fats reduces cardiovascular risk.

## Micronutrients

Vitamins and minerals regulate thousands of biochemical processes. Deficiencies often develop slowly and may present as fatigue, cognitive decline, or impaired wound healing before clinical signs appear.

### Vitamin D and Calcium

These two nutrients work synergistically for bone health. Vitamin D is synthesized through skin exposure to UVB light, but many older adults have limited sun exposure. Dietary sources include fortified dairy products, fatty fish, and egg yolks.

## Meal Timing

Distributing protein intake across three meals is more effective for muscle protein synthesis than concentrating it in a single meal. Eating breakfast within two hours of waking helps regulate appetite hormones throughout the day.
