# Research Methodology Fundamentals

## Overview

Research methodology is the systematic framework that guides how a study is designed, conducted, and interpreted. Choosing an appropriate methodology determines what kinds of claims can be made from the results and how strongly those claims can be supported.

## Quantitative Research

Quantitative methods produce numerical data amenable to statistical analysis. Experiments assign participants randomly to conditions (randomized controlled trials) to establish causal relationships. Surveys collect self-reported data from large samples. Observational studies record naturally occurring behavior without intervention.

### Validity and Reliability

Internal validity refers to the confidence that observed effects are caused by the manipulated variable, not confounds. External validity is the degree to which results generalize beyond the study sample. Reliability refers to consistency: a reliable measure produces the same result under the same conditions. High reliability does not guarantee validity.

## Qualitative Research

Qualitative methods generate rich, contextual understanding through observation, interviews, and document analysis. Grounded theory builds theoretical models from data through iterative coding and constant comparison. Ethnography immerses the researcher in a context to understand it from the inside. Thematic analysis identifies patterns of meaning across a dataset.

## Mixed Methods

Mixed methods designs combine quantitative and qualitative approaches. Sequential exploratory designs begin with qualitative inquiry to generate hypotheses, then test them quantitatively. Sequential explanatory designs collect quantitative data first, then use qualitative data to explain unexpected findings.

## Research Ethics

All research involving human participants requires ethical approval. Informed consent must be obtained voluntarily, without coercion. Participant data must be anonymized or pseudonymized. The principle of non-maleficence — do no harm — takes precedence over scientific value. Vulnerable populations require additional protections.
