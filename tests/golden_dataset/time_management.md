# Time Management Strategies

## Overview

Time management is the discipline of allocating time to activities in proportion to their importance rather than their urgency or visibility. Most people operate in a reactive mode, responding to demands as they arrive. Intentional time management shifts the locus of control from external demands to personal priorities.

## Planning Frameworks

### Time Blocking

Time blocking assigns specific activities to fixed slots in the calendar. By treating planned work as appointments, it resists interruption and prevents the schedule from being colonized by small reactive tasks. Deep work — cognitively demanding work requiring sustained concentration — is scheduled in blocks of at least 90 minutes to allow the warm-up period before full cognitive capacity is reached.

### Weekly Review

A weekly review surveys the past week's accomplishments, processes inboxes, and plans the upcoming week. It typically takes 30 to 60 minutes and involves reviewing all active projects, capturing open loops, and assigning specific outcomes to specific days. The review is the maintenance habit that keeps a productivity system functioning.

## Prioritization Methods

### Eisenhower Matrix

The Eisenhower matrix categorizes tasks by urgency and importance. Urgent and important tasks (crises) require immediate attention. Important but not urgent tasks (strategic work) should be scheduled. Urgent but not important tasks should be delegated. Neither urgent nor important tasks should be eliminated. Most high performers spend the majority of their time in the important-but-not-urgent quadrant.

### MoSCoW Prioritization

MoSCoW labels tasks as Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, or Won't Have. Applied to a project's task list, it makes explicit what is genuinely essential versus aspirational. Ruthlessly restricting the Must Have list reduces scope creep and improves delivery reliability.

## Managing Interruptions

Research shows that after an interruption, it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully return to a complex task. Batching email and messaging responses into two or three daily sessions reduces interruption frequency. Communicating availability windows to colleagues aligns expectations without social friction.

## Time Auditing

Before optimizing time allocation, understanding current allocation is essential. Time tracking for two weeks reveals where time actually goes versus where it is believed to go. The gap between perceived and actual allocation is almost always surprising.
