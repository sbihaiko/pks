# Citation Management for Researchers

## Overview

Citation management software organizes references, generates formatted citations, and prevents bibliographic errors. As research projects grow, manual reference tracking becomes error-prone and unsustainable. A well-maintained reference library is a durable scholarly asset that persists across papers, projects, and career stages.

## Reference Managers

### Zotero

Zotero is free, open-source, and integrates with browsers through a one-click capture plugin. It stores PDFs alongside metadata, supports collaborative group libraries, and synchronizes across devices via the cloud. The word processor plugin inserts citations and automatically generates formatted bibliographies in any installed citation style.

### Mendeley

Mendeley combines reference management with academic social networking. Its PDF annotation tools allow inline notes that are linked to references. Institutional licenses often provide additional storage and features. Mendeley's collaboration features make it practical for research teams with shared reading lists.

## Metadata Quality

Imported metadata is often incomplete or incorrect. DOI lookups and manual field verification are essential when accuracy matters. Missing page numbers, incorrect author name formats, and wrong publication years in imported records produce incorrect citations. The garbage-in, garbage-out principle applies fully.

## Citation Styles

Different disciplines and journals mandate different citation styles. APA (author-date) is standard in social sciences. Chicago Notes-Bibliography is common in humanities. Vancouver (numbered) is used in medicine. IEEE (numbered) is used in engineering. Reference managers store style files (CSL) for thousands of journals and update them as styles change.

## Annotation and Knowledge Synthesis

Beyond organizing references, modern reference managers support annotation and tagging. Tagging papers by theme, methodology, or relevance score builds a personal knowledge base. Reviewing annotations during writing surfaces relevant material without re-reading entire papers.
