# Physical Activity Guidelines

## Overview

Regular physical activity is associated with reduced risk of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, certain cancers, depression, and cognitive decline. It is one of the few interventions that simultaneously improves nearly every health outcome. The challenge is not knowing that exercise is beneficial—it is sustaining the behavior over time.

## Recommended Volumes

The World Health Organization recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week, combined with muscle-strengthening activities on two or more days.

### Intensity Definitions

Moderate intensity corresponds to a brisk walk where conversation is possible but singing is not. Vigorous intensity includes jogging, cycling at speed, or swimming laps. Wearable devices that estimate heart rate zones can help individuals calibrate their effort accurately.

## Types of Exercise

### Aerobic Training

Aerobic exercise improves cardiovascular efficiency, lowers resting blood pressure, and enhances insulin sensitivity. Walking, cycling, swimming, and dancing are low-impact options suitable for older adults or those with joint limitations.

### Resistance Training

Resistance training preserves muscle mass and bone density, both of which decline with age. Effective programs target all major muscle groups two to three times per week with progressive overload—gradually increasing resistance or volume as strength improves.

### Balance and Flexibility

Falls are a leading cause of injury in older adults. Balance training through activities like tai chi or single-leg standing exercises reduces fall risk. Flexibility work through stretching maintains range of motion and reduces injury susceptibility.

## Behavior Change

Habit formation is central to long-term exercise adherence. Attaching exercise to an existing daily anchor—such as walking immediately after breakfast—reduces the decision load that leads to skipping sessions. Social accountability through a walking partner or group class also significantly improves consistency.
